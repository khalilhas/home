Current position

Ph.D. Student at LCPMR Lab, Sorbonne University

    The purpose of my Ph.D. is to detect and quantifiy lithium in solids using an electron probe microanalyzer or scanning electron microscope (SEM). The goal is to develop a technique that is more efficient, accessible, and non-destructive. With the increasing importance of lithium in both economic and scientific realms, there is a significant demand for accurate analysis of lithium distribution at high spatial resolution. This proposed method offers the advantages of being non-destructive, cost-effective, and requiring simpler sample preparation. It has great potential for global implementation, particularly in laboratories equipped with EPMA instruments, which are widely available and more affordable than synchrotron radiation facilities.
